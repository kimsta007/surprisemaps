Introduction

In map visualizations, uncertainty can be encoded using Value Suppressing Uncertainty Palettes (VSUPs). VSUPs help reduce the complexity associated with quantifying uncertainty, by associating an observed rate with a probabilistic value.

For this study we compute a surprise value to color counties based on whether their sales are far enough from expected values so as to be considered surprising.

For example, a small county with 80 sales where there is a total population of 100 people might not be considered as "surprising" as a large county with 80,000 sales with a total population of 100,000 people. Similarly, a small county with 10 sales with a population of 100 might not be colored as "surprising" as a large county with 20,000 sales from a population of 100,000. In other words, we expect smaller counties to vary more in their rates than larger counties, and factor this into the map coloring.
